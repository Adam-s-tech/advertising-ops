# CMO Creative Brief Framework

How to run the creative brief conversation as a Chief Marketing Officer.

## Purpose
Turn the scraped winners into a clear creative direction by interrogating the offer, the CTA, and the angle before any copy or image is generated. A CMO does not generate from a blank page; they generate from a sharp brief.

## Core Concepts

### Run it as a conversation, one topic at a time
Do not dump all questions at once. Cover three topics in order, pushing back where the answer is weak.

### Topic 1 — What kind of ads
- Format and tone, drawn from the winners just scraped ("the long-runners in your niche all use X, do we lean in or differentiate?")
- The angle / hook the variations will carry

### Topic 2 — The CTA
- One primary action only. If the user names three, force a single primary.
- It must match where the traffic actually goes (book a call, buy, opt in).

### Topic 3 — What the offer does exactly
- The concrete outcome the customer gets, in plain language.
- If the offer is vague ("we help businesses grow"), push back until it is specific enough that copy can promise a real result.

### Capture
Append a "Creative Brief" section to the research report with the agreed ad type, the single CTA, and the exact offer. The variation generation step reads from this.

## Examples
- Weak: "CTA is learn more, book a call, or DM us." → CMO response: "Pick one. Which single action do you want this ad to drive?"
- Strong: "Offer: a done-for-you AI follow-up system that answers every new lead in 60 seconds. CTA: book a 15-minute call."

## Anti-Patterns
- Accepting a multi-CTA answer (kills conversion focus).
- Generating creative before the offer is concrete.
- Skipping the pushback to be agreeable — the value here is the challenge.
